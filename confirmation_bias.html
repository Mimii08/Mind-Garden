What is Confirmation Bias?
Your brain naturally seeks information that confirms what you already believe — and ignores what doesn't. This is called Confirmation Bias, and it affects everyone, every day.

The Science
Confirmation bias was first studied by psychologist Peter Wason in 1960. His research showed people naturally test hypotheses by looking for confirming evidence rather than disproving evidence — a pattern that affects everything from news consumption to medical diagnosis.
